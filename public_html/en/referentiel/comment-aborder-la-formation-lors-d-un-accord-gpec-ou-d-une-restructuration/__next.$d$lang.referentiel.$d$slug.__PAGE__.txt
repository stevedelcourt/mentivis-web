## Training as an adaptation lever

Workforce planning anticipates occupational evolution and organizes competency adaptation. Training plays a central role: it allows employees to develop toward tomorrow's competencies rather than facing obsolescence. This anticipation, negotiated in an agreement, transforms a future constraint into an action plan.

## The employer's adaptation obligation

The employer has an obligation to adapt employees to their roles and to maintain their employability. In cases of role change or technological evolution, this obligation translates into training actions. Failure to meet this obligation can be invoked in disputes. Training is therefore not only a development tool but also a legal obligation in certain situations.

## Training in economic transitions

In a restructuring, training can constitute an alternative to redundancy, by retraining employees toward maintained or newly created roles. Specific mechanisms, such as FNE-formation, provide financial support for employee training during difficulties or structural transitions. In a job protection plan, training and retraining measures are among the expected levers. Training becomes an instrument of social management.

## Key takeaways

- Workforce planning positions training as an anticipatory competency adaptation lever.
- The employer has an obligation of adaptation and employability maintenance.
- Failure to meet this obligation can be invoked in disputes.
- Training can constitute an alternative to redundancy in restructurings.
- FNE-formation supports training during difficulties or structural transitions.
