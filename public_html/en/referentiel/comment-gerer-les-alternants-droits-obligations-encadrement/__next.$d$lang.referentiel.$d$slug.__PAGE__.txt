## Apprenticeship or professionalization

The apprenticeship contract is primarily aimed at young people and targets a diploma or title registered in the RNCP. The professionalization contract, which is more broadly accessible, targets a recognized qualification and follows branch logic. Both rest on alternation between periods at the training organization and periods at the company, but their rules on duration, remuneration and financing differ.

## Supervising the apprentice

The master apprentice or company tutor transmits competencies and monitors progress. On the organization side, a designated contact ensures liaison with the company and individualized monitoring. Coordination between these stakeholders, formalized through regular meetings and progress reports, is a Qualiopi expectation for CFAs, under the work-study coordination indicator.

## Managing the unpredictable

Absences, pedagogical difficulties and contract terminations are part of the life of a work-study program. The organization must have procedures to prevent dropout, flag disengagement and support transitions. Administrative management, agreements, amendments, OPCO tracking, also conditions financing and must be handled rigorously, as an incomplete file blocks coverage.

## Key takeaways

- The apprenticeship contract targets an RNCP diploma; professionalization targets a branch qualification.
- The master apprentice or tutor handles competency transmission at the company.
- Stakeholder coordination is a Qualiopi expectation for CFAs.
