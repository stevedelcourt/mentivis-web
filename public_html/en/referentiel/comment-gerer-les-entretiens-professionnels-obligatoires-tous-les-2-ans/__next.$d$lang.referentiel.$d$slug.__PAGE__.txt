4:T8fc,The professional interview is a legal obligation stemming from the 2014 act, codified in article L6315-1 of the labor code: the employer must hold it at least every two years with each employee, across all contract types, and conduct a comprehensive review every six years. Distinct from the annual performance appraisal, it focuses on professional development prospects. The framework evolved in late 2025, and recent case law has clarified the conditions for the associated financial penalty.

## The obligation and its scope

The professional interview concerns all employees, on permanent contracts, fixed-term contracts, part-time and work-study. It is devoted to development prospects, in terms of qualifications and employment, and is clearly distinct from the performance appraisal. An interview must also be offered on return from certain extended absences, such as maternity leave, parental leave or a prolonged sick leave. Traceability of these interviews is essential.

## The six-year review

Every six years, a comprehensive review verifies that the employee has had their interviews and has experienced, over the period, training actions and professional or salary progression. This review is the moment when respect for obligations over time is measured. The legal framework for the professional interview was reformed in late 2025, which makes it important to verify the exact modalities currently in force.

## The penalty and its case law

In companies of at least fifty employees, a failure can trigger a corrective top-up of 3,000 euros on the concerned employee's CPF. A ruling by the Cour de cassation of January 21, 2026 specified that this penalty is only due when two failures are cumulatively established: absence of interviews and no training action over the period. This clarification narrows the scope of the automatic penalty, without lifting the obligation to hold the interviews.

## Key takeaways

- The professional interview is mandatory at least every two years, for all employees.
- It is distinct from the annual performance appraisal.
- A comprehensive review takes place every six years.
- The legal framework evolved in late 2025; check the modalities currently in force.
