15:T981,Migrating from one LMS to another without data loss requires methodically preparing the transfer of content, learner data and history, drawing on technical standards to preserve portability. A poorly prepared migration loses content, training histories and user buy-in. Success rests on a prior audit of the existing setup, a phased migration plan, and a testing phase before cutover. Content portability, guaranteed by standards, is a central concern.

## Auditing the existing setup before migrating

A migration starts with an inventory: which content, in which formats, which learner data, what training history, what integrations with other systems. This audit reveals what is portable, what will need to be rebuilt, and the risks. Migrating without this inventory exposes you to discovering losses once the cutover is done, when it is too late to prevent them.

## Preserving portability through standards

Content compliant with standards such as SCORM or xAPI is in principle portable from one platform to another. Content created in an LMS's proprietary format may be difficult to export, creating lock-in. Migration is an opportunity to measure this lock-in and, going forward, to favor content in standard formats. Learner data and history require particular care, as their export format varies by platform.

## Migrating in phases and testing

A successful migration proceeds in phases rather than as a hard cutover. A plan defines the order of transfer, a testing phase verifies the integrity of content and data on the new platform, and a transition period limits risk. Accompanying users, trainers and learners, toward the new tool conditions buy-in: a technically successful migration that is poorly managed may fail through user rejection.

## Key takeaways

- Migration starts with an audit of the existing setup: content, data, integrations.
- Standards-compliant content is portable; proprietary formats create lock-in.
- Learner data and history require particular care in export.
- Phased migration with a testing phase limits risk.
- Accompanying users conditions buy-in for the new tool.
