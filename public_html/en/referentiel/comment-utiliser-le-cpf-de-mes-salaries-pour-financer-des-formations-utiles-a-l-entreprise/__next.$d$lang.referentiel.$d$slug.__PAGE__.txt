## The CPF belongs to the employee

The personal training account is an individual right attached to the employee, activated at their sole initiative. The employer cannot impose it. Any logic of using the CPF in the company's interest therefore rests on employee buy-in and genuine alignment of interests, not on constraint. The 100-euro co-payment, unless topped up, is part of the employee's equation.

## Top-up as a lever

Top-up funding allows the employer to add funds to the employee's CPF rights to finance training. When training serves both the employee's project and the company's need, top-up creates effective shared financing. A company agreement can organize a top-up policy, giving a clear and readable framework.

## Co-constructing projects

The most productive approach is co-construction: identifying training that develops the employee's employability while responding to a company need. This approach, more durable than one-off mobilization, requires dialogue during professional interviews and a formalized policy. It transforms the CPF from an isolated individual right into a collective lever for skills development.

## Key takeaways

- The CPF belongs to the employee and is activated at their sole initiative.
- Top-up allows the employer to supplement rights for shared training.
- A company agreement can organize a top-up policy.
- Co-construction aligns employee employability and company need.
