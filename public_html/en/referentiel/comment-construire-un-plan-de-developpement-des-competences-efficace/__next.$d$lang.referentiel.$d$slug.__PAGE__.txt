The plan has replaced the former training plan and groups all training actions decided by the employer. ## Gathering individual and collective needs

Gathering crosses two sources. Individual needs come from professional interviews and employee requests. Collective needs are driven by strategy, occupational evolution and company projects. A credible plan starts from the latter, which give it direction, while integrating the former, which sustain engagement.

## Prioritizing and arbitrating

Not all needs can be addressed. Prioritization arbitrates according to impact on company priorities and urgency. Budget arbitration integrates mobilizable financing, OPCO, topped-up CPF, specific mechanisms, which determines the volume of actions achievable. A plan is built under resource constraints, not as a wish list.

## Steering by indicators

The plan is formalized over a multi-year horizon, with a timeline and monitoring indicators. In companies with a works council, the plan is subject to consultation. Steering tracks action implementation and measures effects, which feeds the next plan. A plan that is not steered remains a document, not a lever.

## Key takeaways

- The plan connects the company's strategy to the competencies to develop.
- It crosses collective strategic needs and individual needs.
- Prioritization arbitrates according to impact and urgency, under budget constraint.
- The works council is consulted in companies with representative bodies.
