# 1. How to obtain the Qualiopi certification

The Qualiopi certification is awarded following an audit conducted by a certification body accredited by Cofrac, which verifies compliance with the 7 criteria and 32 indicators of the National Quality Reference Framework. The process runs in four stages: assembling a file of evidence, initial audit, resolution of any non-conformities, then a surveillance and renewal cycle. A single major non-conformity is enough to block certification. The certification has been mandatory since January 1, 2022 for access to public and pooled funding.

## Why the certification is mandatory

The National Quality Reference Framework was created by decree n°2019-565 of June 6, 2019, under the Avenir Professionnel act. Since January 1, 2022, Qualiopi conditions access to funding through OPCOs, the CPF, France Travail, regions and Agefiph. An organization without certification can continue to train, but its services are no longer eligible for financing through these circuits, which closes off most of the solvent market.

## The steps of the process

Preparation begins with an understanding of the reference framework and the assembly of an evidence file organized by criterion. The official reading guide, version 9 of January 8, 2024, specifies for each indicator the expected level and examples of acceptable evidence.

The choice of certifier comes next. The organization enters into a contract with an accredited certifier from the list published by Cofrac. Fees vary according to the size of the structure and the scope of its activities.

The initial audit takes place on-site or remotely. The auditor proceeds through interviews, document review and direct observation. Each indicator is assessed as conforming, in minor non-conformity, or in major non-conformity.

The certification lifecycle then runs for three years, with a surveillance audit between the fourteenth and twenty-second month, followed by a renewal audit.

## The evidence that makes the difference

The auditor looks for dated, versioned and traceable evidence. A timestamped LMS export, a dated satisfaction questionnaire or a maintained complaints register carries more weight than a static document. The most effective method is to organize evidence in a folder structure by criterion, so that each requirement finds its justification immediately.

## Key takeaways

- Qualiopi is issued by a Cofrac-accredited certifier, based on the 7 criteria and 32 indicators of the RNQ.
- It has been mandatory since January 1, 2022 for access to public and pooled funding.
- The reference reading guide is version 9 of January 8, 2024.
- A single major non-conformity blocks certification.
- Evidence must be dated, versioned and traceable.

# 2. How to create a CFA in France in 2026

Creating an apprenticeship training center requires defining a legal structure, filing a declaration of activity with the DREETS of your region, obtaining a UAI code from the academic services, achieving Qualiopi compliance and organizing the delivery of certified training. Since the Avenir Professionnel act, all CFAs are subject to the obligation to file a declaration of activity. The declaration must be filed within three months of the first contract.

## The framework since the Avenir Professionnel act

The act of September 5, 2018 liberalized the creation of CFAs. It is no longer necessary to obtain a prior agreement with the region: any organization meeting the regulatory requirements can open a CFA. This opening is accompanied by strengthened requirements, notably Qualiopi certification on the apprenticeship scope, which requires CFAs to satisfy 32 indicators, ten more than other training providers.

## Administrative formalities

The legal structure is defined first. The CFA can be a dedicated entity or an activity within an existing organization. A company-based CFA is not required to mention apprenticeship training activity in its articles of association.

The declaration of activity is filed with the DREETS of the registered office address. It gives the right to the activity registration number, which becomes mandatory on contracts from the second one onward.

The UAI code is obtained from the academic services of the Education Ministry; it identifies the institution.

Regulatory compliance specific to CFAs is then put in place, including the indicators specific to apprenticeship: certification attainment rates, coordination of work-study, citizenship education, and information on apprentice rights and duties.

## The economics of apprenticeship

Financing rests on the coverage of contracts by OPCOs, according to levels set by France Compétences. The CFA invoices the OPCO on the basis of a coherent file and compliant supporting documents. Mastery of the circuit from contract to invoice to payment directly determines the cash position of the organization. Apprenticeship financing rules change regularly, which requires active monitoring.

## Key takeaways

- Since the Avenir Professionnel act, creating a CFA no longer requires a prior regional agreement.
- The declaration of activity is filed with the DREETS within three months of the first contract.
- The UAI code is issued by the academic services.
- CFAs must satisfy the 32 Qualiopi indicators.
- Financing goes through OPCO coverage according to France Compétences levels.

# 3. How to finance professional training with no out-of-pocket cost

Financing training with no out-of-pocket cost requires mobilizing the right mechanism according to the beneficiary's profile: CPF for an active employee on their own initiative, training plan and OPCO for an employee on the employer's decision, Pro-A for internal retraining, France Travail for a job seeker. The key is identifying the right funder and assembling a compliant file before the training begins, as coverage cannot be claimed retroactively.

## Identifying the right mechanism

The mechanism depends on the status and the initiator. For an employee whose training is decided by the employer, the skills development plan mobilizes funds from the branch OPCO. For an active employee training on their own initiative, the CPF finances certified eligible training. For an internal retraining or promotion, Pro-A combines work-study and contract continuity. For a job seeker, France Travail and regions step in.

## Coverage circuits

The CPF is activated via Mon Compte Formation. Since the introduction of a 100-euro co-payment per file, the holder contributes to the cost unless a third party, employer or France Travail, provides top-up funding.

The OPCO finances on the basis of a training agreement and a coverage file submitted before the start. Coverage levels are set by branch.

Complementary mechanisms, such as FNE-formation in cases of economic difficulty, or regional subsidies, can be added depending on the situation.

## Securing the file in advance

Coverage is prepared before training begins. A file submitted late, a non-compliant agreement, or an ineligible training action closes off funding. The organization benefits from verifying the eligibility of each action, anticipating OPCO instruction deadlines, and retaining supporting documents.

## Key takeaways

- The financing mechanism depends on the beneficiary's status and the initiator.
- The CPF goes through Mon Compte Formation with a 100-euro co-payment unless topped up.
- The OPCO finances on agreement and file submitted before the start.
- Coverage levels are set by branch.
- Coverage is prepared in advance and cannot be claimed retroactively.

# 4. How to create a training program compliant with Qualiopi

A Qualiopi-compliant training program rests on criterion 2 of the National Quality Reference Framework: it must connect a needs analysis, objectives formulated as measurable competencies, structured content organized into sequences, and coherent assessment modalities. The document the auditor expects is the pedagogical session plan, which covers several indicators on its own. Without this documented logical chain, the organization risks a major non-conformity, sufficient to block certification.

## The regulatory framework to respect

The National Quality Reference Framework was created by decree n°2019-565 of June 6, 2019, under the Avenir Professionnel act. It has been mandatory since January 1, 2022 for any organization seeking access to public or pooled funding.

The reference framework contains 7 criteria and 32 indicators. The design of a program falls mainly under criterion 2, which concerns the precise identification of objectives and the adaptation of the service to the target audience. The reading guide in force, version 9 of January 8, 2024, specifies for each indicator the expected level and examples of acceptable evidence.

## The four structuring elements

Needs analysis opens the chain. Before designing, the organization gathers the expectations of the beneficiary and, where applicable, the funder. Expected evidence: positioning questionnaire, pre-training interview, intake form.

Objectives come next. They must be formulated in terms of verifiable competencies. Vague formulations such as "raise awareness of" are not sufficient. The auditor expects action verbs that can be evaluated: design, manage, write, diagnose.

Content and modalities structure the pathway: breakdown into sequences, methods, materials, duration and time sequencing aligned with objectives.

Entry-level positioning closes the loop, through an assessment of each learner's level before the program begins.

## The document that makes the difference at audit

The pedagogical session plan links the greatest number of indicators. It brings together objectives, content, methods, materials, assessments and sequencing in a single roadmap, which serves both as a guide for the trainer and as evidence for the auditor. Evidence must be dated, versioned and traceable.

## Key takeaways

- Compliance of a program is decided on criterion 2 of the RNQ.
- Objectives must be formulated as verifiable competencies.
- The pedagogical session plan is the central document expected at audit.
- The reference reading guide is version 9 of January 8, 2024.
- A single major non-conformity can block certification.

# 5. How to register your professional training activity

The declaration of activity is filed with the DREETS of the registered office address, within three months of the conclusion of the first training agreement or the first training contract. It gives the right to the activity registration number, a mandatory mention on all contracts and agreements from the second one onward. This formality is mandatory whether the training activity is carried out as a primary or secondary activity, on pain of sanctions that can extend to cancellation of the registration number.

## The principle of the declaration

Any provider of actions contributing to skills development must register. The first agreement or first contract is concluded without a number, since the declaration follows. From the second onward, the activity registration number is mandatory on all contractual documents.

Organizations delivering training exclusively to their own employees internally are exempt from the declaration, except for company-based CFAs.

## The procedure with the DREETS

The competent DREETS is the one in the location of the registered office, main establishment or effective management. The file includes the organization's identification details, the nature of the services, and, depending on the case, supporting documents for the first action delivered. The administration may request a document relating to the first training service delivered, the target audience or the nature of the training.

## Obligations that follow the declaration

The declaration opens ongoing obligations. The pedagogical and financial report must be submitted each year; failure to transmit it may result in cancellation of the activity registration number. Contractual documents must comply with mandatory mentions, including the registration number and assessment modalities. The declaration does not constitute state approval or a quality endorsement, which must be made clear in communications.

## Key takeaways

- The declaration is filed with the DREETS within three months of the first contract.
- It gives the right to the activity registration number, mandatory from the second contract onward.
- Internal training for the organization's own employees is exempt, except for company-based CFAs.
- The annual pedagogical and financial report is mandatory.
- Failure to submit the report may result in cancellation of the number.

# 6. How to design a compliant competency assessment (bilan de compétences)

A compliant competency assessment adheres to a precise regulatory framework: it runs in three mandatory phases, preliminary, investigation and conclusion, it is delivered by a Qualiopi-certified provider for the competency assessment category, and it results in a synthesis document given exclusively to the beneficiary. Its duration does not exceed twenty-four hours spread over several weeks. It is distinguished from coaching or career guidance by its legal framework and its purpose of developing a professional project.

## The legal framework

The competency assessment is an action contributing to skills development, subject to the National Quality Reference Framework. The provider must be Qualiopi-certified in the corresponding category for the service to be eligible for CPF financing or other circuits. Confidentiality is an obligation: the detailed results and synthesis document belong solely to the beneficiary.

## The three mandatory phases

The preliminary phase analyzes the request, defines needs and informs the beneficiary about the conditions and methods of the assessment.

The investigation phase explores motivations, competencies, aptitudes and development potential, using evaluation and analysis tools.

The conclusion phase enables the beneficiary to assimilate the results, identify success factors for their project and define its steps. It results in the synthesis document.

## What distinguishes an assessment from coaching

The competency assessment is governed by the labor code, which is not the case for coaching or career guidance. This framework grounds its CPF eligibility and imposes the three-phase structure, capped duration and confidentiality. A provider offering an assessment without respecting this framework cannot have it financed as such.

## Key takeaways

- The competency assessment runs in three phases: preliminary, investigation, conclusion.
- The provider must be Qualiopi-certified for the competency assessment category.
- Duration does not exceed twenty-four hours.
- The synthesis document is given to the beneficiary alone.
- Confidentiality is a legal obligation, not a commercial option.

# 7. How to build a RNCP certification file

Building a RNCP registration file requires submitting to France Compétences, through its online procedure, a file structured around a feasibility study and three reference frameworks: activities, competencies, and assessment. The professional certification commission examines the relevance of the certification against the labor market and the coherence of the competency blocks. The instruction period is approximately six months, and it is recommended to submit seven to nine months before any deadline.

## The framework and the registers

France Compétences manages two registers: the RNCP, for certifications attesting to an occupation, and the Répertoire Spécifique, for complementary competencies. Registration in the RNCP conditions access to certain types of financing and to listing on Mon Compte Formation for certified training programs.

## The structure of the file

The feasibility study opens the file. It demonstrates the utility of the certification and its alignment with labor market needs, through analysis of professional situations.

The activity reference framework describes the professional situations targeted. The competency reference framework translates them into evaluable competencies, organized into coherent blocks. The assessment reference framework defines the modalities for verifying each block.

The overall coherence between these reference frameworks and the quality of the examination procedures are central assessment criteria. Access through VAE must also be provided for.

## Deadlines, rates and points of vigilance

The instruction period is approximately six months; a submission seven to nine months in advance secures deadlines. The RNCP acceptance rate has historically sat around two thirds, significantly higher than for the Répertoire Spécifique, where unfamiliarity with expectations weighs on decisions. Literal reproduction of an existing reference framework results in rejection without examination. An updated version of the France Compétences vademecum frames the expectations and procedures.

## Key takeaways

- Submission is made to France Compétences via its online procedure.
- The file includes a feasibility study and three reference frameworks: activities, competencies, assessment.
- The instruction period is approximately six months.
- It is recommended to submit seven to nine months before the deadline.
- Literal reproduction of an existing reference framework results in rejection.

# 8. How to calculate the pedagogical cost of a training program

Establishing the pedagogical cost of a training program consists in adding up direct costs (trainer remuneration, materials, room rental, platform) and a share of indirect costs (administration, sales, overhead), then dividing this total by the number of hours and learners to obtain an hourly cost and a per-learner cost. Pricing is then built by adding a margin to this cost price, taking into account the coverage levels of funders.

## Distinguishing direct and indirect costs

Direct costs are traceable to a specific action: trainer fees or loaded salary, materials design, room rental, platform licenses, travel expenses. Indirect costs cover general operations: administration, sales, pedagogical oversight, permanent premises. An allocation key, often based on hour volume, distributes a share of indirect costs to each action.

## From cost price to pricing

The total cost price divided by hour volume gives the hourly cost. Divided by the number of learners, it gives the per-learner cost, a key indicator for group training where pooling reduces the unit cost.

Pricing adds an operational margin to the cost price. It is also assessed against the market and OPCO coverage levels, which effectively cap the acceptable price for certain actions.

## Managing profitability by module

Tracking cost by module makes it possible to identify profitable actions and those that weigh on the organization. A module with low enrolment may be loss-making despite a high listed price, if direct costs are not covered. Management consists in arbitrating between volume, price and pooling, and deciding whether to maintain, adjust or discontinue an action.

## Key takeaways

- Pedagogical cost adds direct costs and a share of indirect costs.
- Hourly cost and per-learner cost are the two key indicators.
- Pricing adds a margin to the cost price, within the limits of the market and coverage levels.
- Pooling reduces the unit cost of group training.
- Module-level management reveals which actions are genuinely profitable.

# 9. How to manage apprentices: rights, obligations and supervision

Managing apprentices rests on the distinction between the apprenticeship contract and the professionalization contract, on the designation of a master apprentice or tutor, and on compliance with alternation between time at the training center and time at the company. The organization must ensure coordination of stakeholders, individualized pedagogical monitoring, administrative management of contracts with the OPCO, and support in cases of absence or contract termination. These obligations are reinforced for CFAs by the Qualiopi indicators specific to apprenticeship.

## Apprenticeship or professionalization

The apprenticeship contract is primarily aimed at young people and targets a diploma or title registered in the RNCP. The professionalization contract, which is more broadly accessible, targets a recognized qualification and follows branch logic. Both rest on alternation between periods at the training organization and periods at the company, but their rules on duration, remuneration and financing differ.

## Supervising the apprentice

The master apprentice or company tutor transmits competencies and monitors progress. On the organization side, a designated contact ensures liaison with the company and individualized monitoring. Coordination between these stakeholders, formalized through regular meetings and progress reports, is a Qualiopi expectation for CFAs, under the work-study coordination indicator.

## Managing the unpredictable

Absences, pedagogical difficulties and contract terminations are part of the life of a work-study program. The organization must have procedures to prevent dropout, flag disengagement and support transitions. Administrative management, agreements, amendments, OPCO tracking, also conditions financing and must be handled rigorously, as an incomplete file blocks coverage.

## Key takeaways

- The apprenticeship contract targets an RNCP diploma; professionalization targets a branch qualification.
- The master apprentice or tutor handles competency transmission at the company.
- Stakeholder coordination is a Qualiopi expectation for CFAs.
- Dropout prevention procedures must be in place.
- Administrative rigor conditions OPCO coverage.

# 10. How to assess learner outcomes in a valid and defensible way

Assessing learner outcomes in a valid way requires distinguishing three assessment functions, formative to adjust during the course, summative to measure a level achieved, and certifying to validate a certification, and aligning each modality with the announced competency objectives. Modalities must be documented, traceable and compliant with certifier expectations for certified training. Professional simulations and criteria-based grids are the most robust evidence at audit.

## The three functions of assessment

Formative assessment occurs during the course to track progress and adjust the pedagogy. It does not grade, it regulates. Summative assessment measures a level achieved at a given moment, often at the end of a sequence or course. Certifying assessment, specific to training leading to a certification, validates the acquisition of competency blocks according to the modalities set by the certifier's assessment reference framework.

## Aligning assessment with objectives

A valid assessment measures what it claims to measure. It flows directly from the competency-formulated objectives: each targeted competency has a matching, adapted assessment modality. Training that targets the ability to manage a project cannot make do with a knowledge quiz; it calls for a simulation. This alignment is at the heart of Qualiopi expectations on the achievement of objectives.

## Evidence expected at audit

For certified training, the organization must have procedures to control the organization of examinations and criteria-based assessment grids. Professional simulations, assessed outputs and completed grids constitute the most robust evidence. Results must be traceable and retained. A vague or undocumented assessment system weakens both the organization's certification and the value of the certification it delivers.

## Key takeaways

- Assessment breaks down into three functions: formative, summative, certifying.
- Each modality must be aligned with competency objectives.
- Certified training follows the certifier's assessment reference framework.
- Simulations and criteria-based grids are the most robust evidence.
- Results must be traceable and retained.
