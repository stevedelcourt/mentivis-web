# 11. How to build a training curriculum from scratch

Building a training curriculum from scratch means starting from needs analysis and an analysis of professional situations to arrive at a breakdown into competency blocks, sequenced and associated with assessment modalities. The process chains five steps: needs analysis, definition of target competencies, structuring into blocks, pedagogical sequencing, and alignment with an occupational reference framework or an RNCP certification. Coherence between targeted competencies, content and assessments is the central quality criterion.

## Starting from needs and professional situations

A solid curriculum does not start from available content but from real need. The analysis of professional situations identifies what a professional must be able to do. This analysis grounds the activity reference framework, which describes professional situations, and then the competency reference framework, which translates them into evaluable capabilities.

## Structuring into competency blocks

The breakdown into competency blocks has become the standard. Each block groups a coherent set of competencies, evaluable independently. This structure facilitates alignment with the RNCP, modularization of the offering and partial VAE. Blocks must be internally coherent and cover the full range of targeted activities without redundancy or gap.

## Sequencing and aligning with assessment

Pedagogical sequencing orders learning according to a logical progression, from prerequisites to mastery. Each competency has a corresponding adapted assessment modality, defined in the assessment reference framework. It is the alignment between competencies, content and assessments that makes the curriculum valid: a targeted competency that is not assessed, or an assessment without a corresponding objective, signals an inconsistency that a certifier or auditor immediately identifies.

## Key takeaways

- A curriculum starts from needs and professional situations, not from existing content.
- It chains activity, competency and assessment reference frameworks.
- The breakdown into competency blocks is the standard and facilitates RNCP and VAE.
- Sequencing orders learning according to a logical progression.
- Coherence between competencies, content and assessments grounds validity.

# 12. How to attract apprentices and trainees in 2026

Attracting apprentices and trainees rests on an acquisition strategy combining targeted digital visibility, presence on orientation platforms, partnerships with prescribing institutions and social proof through former participant testimonials. The challenge is no longer simply filling sessions, but building a conversion funnel that turns awareness into qualified applications and then into enrolments. The quality of the company relationships directly determines the volume of work-study learners recruited.

## Building visibility in the right places

Apprenticeship and training candidates research online before applying. Structured presence combines organic search ranking on occupational queries, targeted campaigns on the platforms frequented by target audiences, and up-to-date listings on orientation platforms. Consistency between the promise displayed and the reality of the training supports conversion rates.

## Mobilizing prescribers

Upstream institutions, secondary schools, universities, orientation programs, are decisive prescribers. Active partnerships, interventions and presence at recruitment events feed a steady flow of applications. On the company side, the ability to propose job-ready work-study candidates builds loyalty among partners and secures work-study placements.

## Converting awareness into enrolments

Visibility is not enough without a conversion funnel. A clear landing page, a smooth application form and organized follow-up turn interest into action. Testimonials from former participants and results indicators, placement rates, satisfaction rates, are powerful social proof tools, all the more so as they also form part of Qualiopi transparency obligations.

## Key takeaways

- Acquisition combines digital visibility, orientation platforms and prescribers.
- Consistency between promise and reality supports conversion.
- Partnerships with upstream institutions feed the application pipeline.
- A clear conversion funnel turns interest into enrolment.
- Testimonials and results indicators serve as social proof.

# 13. How to obtain and use apprenticeship tax funds

Eligible institutions receive the apprenticeship tax balance, set at 0.09 percent of company payroll, through the SOLTéA platform managed by the Caisse des dépôts. Since the reform, employers no longer pay institutions directly: they declare the balance via April's DSN payroll filing, then designate their beneficiaries on SOLTéA during an annual window. To benefit, an institution must appear on the list of eligible recipients and actively build visibility with companies.

## The mechanism since the reform

The apprenticeship tax comprises a main portion, which finances apprenticeship through OPCOs, and a balance of 0.09 percent dedicated to initial technological and vocational training and to employment inclusion. This balance is declared and paid via the April DSN, collected by Urssaf or MSA, then paid over to the Caisse des dépôts which redistributes it according to designations made on SOLTéA. No direct cash payment to institutions is possible outside the platform.

## The SOLTéA calendar

The allocation campaign opens each spring and runs across two designation windows, with staggered payments to designated institutions. Funds not designated by employers by the closing date are allocated by regulation, primarily toward training preparing for occupations in shortage in the employer's region. Recipient institutions must enter their details and bank account on the platform to receive funds.

## Capturing the balance as an institution

Appearing on the list of eligible institutions is the access condition. Beyond that, capturing the balance requires active outreach to companies: making yourself known, explaining the designation procedure and building relationships with local employers, since they are the ones who designate recipients. In-kind contributions, equipment or materials, remain possible directly but outside the platform.

## Key takeaways

- The apprenticeship tax balance is set at 0.09 percent of payroll.
- It transits through SOLTéA, managed by the Caisse des dépôts; no direct cash payment is possible.
- Employers declare via April's DSN then designate their beneficiaries.
- Undesignated funds are allocated by regulation toward occupations in shortage.
- An institution must be eligible and actively engage companies.

# 14. How to digitize training without losing pedagogical quality

Digitizing training without degrading quality requires rethinking the pedagogy for remote delivery rather than transposing face-to-face content unchanged. Success rests on three levers: a pedagogical design adapted to the cognitive load of online learning, a choice of modalities coherent with the objectives, and an engagement mechanism that sustains completion rates. Remote learning recognized by funders also requires traceable records of attendance and progress.

## Rethinking rather than transposing

The common mistake is to film a face-to-face session and broadcast it as-is. Remote learning follows different rules: shorter sequences, varied formats, immediate feedback, spaced practice. Pedagogical design for the screen accounts for cognitive load and the fact that attention in self-directed settings differs from that in a room.

## Choosing the right modalities

Remote learning divides into synchronous, virtual classroom, and asynchronous, e-learning modules. The choice depends on the objectives: a relational competency calls for synchronous interaction, a procedural skill lends itself to asynchronous. The hybrid format combines both. Each modality must serve the objective, not the other way around.

## Sustaining engagement and tracking progress

Completion rate is the weak point of remote learning. Notifications, reminders, cohorts, human accompaniment and the manager's role all sustain engagement. For funded training, OPCO recognition requires traceability: attendance, connection time, progress and assessment must all be documented. A timestamped platform export constitutes acceptable evidence at audit.

## Key takeaways

- Digitizing means rethinking the pedagogy, not transposing face-to-face content.
- Design accounts for cognitive load and self-directed attention.
- The choice between synchronous, asynchronous or hybrid flows from the objectives.
- Engagement is sustained by reminders, cohorts and human accompaniment.
- Traceability of attendance and progress is required for funded training.

# 15. How to manage the relationship with OPCOs day to day

Managing the OPCO relationship rests on mastery of three elements: knowing your contact and the branch concerned, submitting compliant coverage files before training begins, and rigorously tracking invoicing and reimbursements. OPCOs finance according to coverage levels set by branch, on the basis of coherent files. An incomplete or late-submitted file blocks payment and weighs on the organization's cash position.

## Identifying the right OPCO and contact

Eleven OPCOs cover the professional branches. Each company falls under an OPCO according to its collective agreement. France Compétences provides a tool to identify the competent OPCO. On the organization side, knowing branch contacts and their procedures smooths file processing.

## Submitting compliant files

Coverage is requested before the training action begins, on the basis of a training agreement and the required documents. Coverage levels are set by branch and cap financing. A file must be coherent, complete and submitted in advance: instruction deadlines cannot be caught up, and an action that has already started without prior agreement may remain at the cost of the organization or the client.

## Tracking invoicing and reimbursements

The circuit from contract to invoice to reimbursement determines cash flow. Invoicing rests on attendance and delivery supporting documents. Coverage refusals, frequent when a document is missing or a non-conformity is present, must be handled without delay. Structured tracking, with organized follow-up, reduces payment delays and losses.

## Key takeaways

- Each company falls under an OPCO according to its branch; a France Compétences tool identifies it.
- Coverage is requested before the training action begins.
- Coverage levels are set by branch and cap financing.
- Invoicing rests on attendance and delivery supporting documents.
- Structured tracking reduces refusals and payment delays.

# 16. How to draft a legally compliant training agreement

A compliant training agreement respects the mandatory mentions set out in article L6353-1 of the labor code: title, objectives, content, means, duration, dates, modalities for delivery and assessment, price and payment terms. It is distinguished from a purchase order or a quote by its full contractual value. An incomplete agreement weakens the client relationship, coverage by funders and the Qualiopi compliance of the organization.

## The mandatory mentions

Article L6353-1 frames the content of the agreement concluded between the organization and the training buyer. It specifies the title, nature, objectives and content of the action, the pedagogical and technical means, duration and dates, modalities for delivery, monitoring and assessment of results, as well as price and payment terms. These elements directly overlap with Qualiopi expectations on public information and the definition of objectives.

## Agreement, contract, purchase order

The training agreement binds the organization to a legal entity, most often a company. The training contract applies when an individual finances their own training; it contains additional protective provisions, including a right of withdrawal. A purchase order or quote does not replace the agreement: it may accompany it but does not carry the same contractual value.

## Securing edge cases

The agreement must anticipate contingencies: conditions for cancellation, postponement, withdrawal, and liability in case of non-performance. General terms and conditions cover these points. A well-constructed agreement protects the organization in case of dispute and secures coverage by funders, who require a compliant agreement to reimburse.

## Key takeaways

- The agreement follows the mandatory mentions of article L6353-1.
- It is distinguished from the individual contract, which includes a right of withdrawal.
- A purchase order does not replace the agreement.
- Cancellation, postponement and withdrawal conditions must be provided for.
- A compliant agreement conditions coverage by funders.

# 17. How to build a skills development plan for a corporate client

Building a skills development plan for a corporate client consists in gathering needs, prioritizing them against the company's strategy, arbitrating budget and financing, then formalizing a multi-year action plan steered by indicators. The organization adds consulting value beyond simple training sales: it helps the company connect its business priorities to the competencies to develop, which builds a lasting relationship.

## Gathering and prioritizing needs

Needs gathering crosses two sources: individual needs, drawn from professional interviews and employee requests, and collective needs, driven by strategy and occupational evolution. Prioritization arbitrates between these needs according to impact and urgency. A credible plan distinguishes the essential from the secondary rather than stacking requests.

## Arbitrating budget and financing

The plan accounts for available budget and mobilizable financing: OPCO funds, CPF top-ups, specific programs. The organization advises on financing optimization, which increases the volume of actions achievable at a constant budget. This arbitration turns a budget constraint into a realistic plan.

## Formalizing and steering

The plan is formalized over a multi-year horizon, with a timeline, objectives and monitoring indicators. For companies with representative bodies, the plan is subject to a works council consultation. Steering tracks the implementation of actions and measures their effect, which feeds the next plan and demonstrates the value of the support.

## Key takeaways

- The plan crosses individual needs and collective strategic needs.
- Prioritization arbitrates according to impact and urgency.
- Financing optimization increases the volume of actions at a constant budget.
- The plan is formalized over a multi-year horizon with indicators.
- The works council is consulted in companies with representative bodies.

# 18. How to create a CPF-eligible training program

Making a training program eligible for the CPF requires that it prepares for an active certification registered in the RNCP or the Répertoire Spécifique, that the organization is Qualiopi-certified, and that it lists the offering on Mon Compte Formation as a certifier or as a partner authorized by a certifier. Eligibility depends not on content alone but on the link to a recognized certification. The 100-euro co-payment per file, unless topped up, is now part of the pricing equation.

## The certification condition

A training program is not CPF-eligible in itself: it is its link to an active certification that opens financing. The certification must be registered in the RNCP, for an occupation, or in the Répertoire Spécifique, for complementary competencies, and must be currently valid. Without an active linked certification, no CPF eligibility is possible.

## Being certifier or partner

The organization that holds the certification is the certifier. Other organizations can deliver training preparing for that certification if they are authorized by the certifier through an authorization agreement. This relationship conditions the right to list the offering on Mon Compte Formation. Qualiopi certification is also a prerequisite for listing.

## Listing and pricing on Mon Compte Formation

Listing is done on the EDOF platform. The offering must display compliant information and results indicators. Pricing is framed and monitored, with abuses potentially resulting in de-listing. Since the introduction of a 100-euro co-payment per file, the holder contributes to the cost unless covered by a third party, which modifies the purchasing decision and must be factored into the pricing positioning.

## Key takeaways

- CPF eligibility depends on a link to an active RNCP or RS certification.
- The organization must be certifier or authorized by a certifier.
- Qualiopi certification is a prerequisite for listing.
- Listing is done on EDOF with compliant information and results.
- A 100-euro co-payment applies unless topped up.

# 19. How to manage the pedagogical quality of your trainers

Managing the pedagogical quality of trainers rests on a complete cycle: recruitment and authorization based on competencies, supervision and support, evaluation through learner feedback at close and at distance, and a trainer development plan. For subcontracted trainers, selection and monitoring engage the organization's responsibility under Qualiopi. The perceived quality of the training depends largely on trainer quality, making it a central management lever.

## Recruiting and authorizing

Recruitment verifies technical and pedagogical competencies. Authorization formalizes the scope of each trainer's intervention. Qualiopi expects evidence of trainer competencies: CVs, diplomas, certificates, experience. This documentation constitutes both an evidence base for audit and a clear framework for the trainer.

## Supervising and evaluating

Supervision accompanies the trainer over time. Close evaluations, at the end of training, and distance evaluations, at a later point, gather learner feedback. This feedback feeds continuous improvement, expected under Qualiopi's quality criterion. Effective use of feedback, not merely its collection, distinguishes genuine management from a formality.

## Developing and managing subcontracting

A trainer development plan sustains quality over time. When the organization uses subcontracted trainers, it remains responsible for quality before the certifier: selection, contractual framing and monitoring of subcontractors are governed, as the Qualiopi reading guide has specified. A poorly framed subcontractor exposes the organization to a non-conformity.

## Key takeaways

- Management follows a cycle: recruitment, authorization, supervision, evaluation, development.
- Qualiopi expects evidence of trainer competencies.
- Close and distance feedback must be used, not merely collected.
- A development plan sustains quality over time.
- The organization remains responsible for the quality of its subcontractors.

# 20. How to manage training subcontracting legally

Managing subcontracting legally requires formalizing a subcontracting agreement that specifies the missions assigned, and understanding that the contracting organization remains responsible for Qualiopi compliance on subcontracted actions. Since version 9 of the reading guide and the decree of December 28, 2023, the scope of indicator verification with the subcontractor depends on the missions defined in the contract. Unframed subcontracting exposes the contracting organization to a non-conformity.

## The subcontracting framework

Recourse to subcontracting is permitted but regulated. A certified organization that assigns all or part of an action to a third party remains responsible for its quality before the certifier. Version 9 of the reading guide, applicable since March 8, 2024, introduced a dedicated chapter on subcontracting, in response to decree n°2023-1350 of December 28, 2023.

## The subcontracting agreement

The subcontracting agreement specifies the missions assigned to the subcontractor. This precision is determinative: when examining a subcontracted action, the certifier verifies compliance with indicators based on the missions defined in the contract. In the absence of a contract specifying the missions, verification covers all indicators of the relevant action category, which broadens the risk.

## The contracting organization's responsibility

The contracting organization must ensure that the subcontractor complies with the indicators falling within its missions. This requires rigorous selection, clear contractual framing and documented monitoring. The traceability of this monitoring constitutes evidence at audit. A failing subcontractor reflects on the contracting organization's certification, which is why strict framing is preferable to blind delegation.

## Key takeaways

- Subcontracting is permitted but the contracting organization remains responsible for quality.
- Version 9 of the reading guide governs subcontracting since March 2024.
- The agreement must specify the missions assigned to the subcontractor.
- Without a contract specifying missions, all indicators of the category are verified.
- Documented monitoring of the subcontractor constitutes evidence at audit.
