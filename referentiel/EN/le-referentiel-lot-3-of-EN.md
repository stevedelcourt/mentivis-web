# 21. How to obtain quality certifications beyond Qualiopi

Beyond Qualiopi, which is mandatory for access to funding, an organization can pursue complementary labels and certifications to strengthen its credibility: sector or branch certifications, international quality management standards, regional labels. These are voluntary and do not open financing rights, but they differentiate on competitive markets and reassure demanding buyers. Their value depends entirely on the recognition they carry with the target clients.

## Qualiopi, the mandatory foundation

Qualiopi remains the inescapable prerequisite, since it conditions access to public and pooled funds. No other certification substitutes for it in this respect. Complementary approaches are therefore built on this foundation, never instead of it.

## The possible complementary certifications

International management standards applied to training attest to a structured quality approach and are sometimes expected by large corporate buyers. Branch certifications and accreditations validate recognized sector expertise. Regional labels or specific listings may open access to certain territorial markets. Each responds to a specific use case.

## Choosing according to client-perceived value

A complementary certification only has value if the target clients recognize it. Before engaging in a costly process, the organization assesses the expected return: market opening, differentiation, requirement from a client segment. Stacking labels without commercial use dilutes the message rather than strengthening it. The choice is managed as an investment, measured by its contribution to acquisition.

## Key takeaways

- Qualiopi remains the mandatory foundation for access to funding.
- Complementary certifications are voluntary and do not open financing rights.
- International standards, branch certifications and regional labels serve distinct use cases.
- The value of a label depends on its recognition by target clients.
- The choice is managed as a commercial investment.

# 22. How to develop an in-company (intra) training activity

Developing an in-company training activity means offering tailored training delivered within a single organization for its own employees, as opposed to open inter-company sessions welcoming participants from different organizations. Intra training rests on a distinct economic model, built on customization, a negotiated flat-fee rather than per-participant pricing, and an account relationship that favors recurrence. Its success depends on the ability to industrialize customization without destroying profitability.

## Intra and inter: two distinct models

Inter-company training brings together participants from several organizations in a catalogue session at a per-person rate. Intra dedicates a session to a single company, tailored, invoiced at a flat fee. Intra offers more predictable margins and a deeper relationship, but requires customization effort and an account-oriented sales force.

## Building the tailored offer

Intra delivers on customization: adaptation to the company's context, its concrete cases, its vocabulary. This adaptation justifies the fee and differentiates from a standard catalogue. The operational challenge is to industrialize this customization, starting from reusable building blocks that are adapted, rather than reconceiving each intervention from scratch, which would destroy profitability.

## Building loyalty through the account relationship

Intra is sold to accounts, not to individuals. A framework agreement, a single point of contact and results tracking nurture a lasting relationship. Satisfaction on a first intervention opens subsequent intra sessions, making the cost of acquisition far lower than for inter training. Loyalty becomes the principal lever of profitable growth.

## Key takeaways

- Intra is dedicated to one company at a flat fee; inter is open and per-participant.
- Intra offers more predictable margins but requires customization.
- Industrializing customization from reusable building blocks protects profitability.
- Intra is sold to accounts through a lasting relationship.
- Loyalty is the principal lever of profitable growth.

# 23. How to support learners with disabilities

Supporting learners with disabilities imposes legal obligations and constitutes a Qualiopi expectation: appointment of a disability coordinator, information on accessibility conditions, pedagogical and material adaptation, and mobilization of specialist stakeholders. Appointing a coordinator is not enough; auditors expect concrete accessibility actions and partnerships with disability sector actors. Support is prepared ahead of enrolment and documented throughout.

## The framework and the Qualiopi obligation

The National Quality Reference Framework contains requirements on the inclusion of disability. Information on accessibility and possible adaptations falls under the criterion on public information. Appointment of a disability coordinator is expected, as is demonstration of concrete actions: without evidence of effective actions, a simple appointment is insufficient.

## The adaptations to provide

Adaptation can be pedagogical, accessible materials, adjusted pace, alternative methods, or material and technical, building accessibility, adapted tools. The organization demonstrates that it accounts for disability situations and compensation needs. These adaptations are planned ahead of delivery, not improvised when a concerned learner arrives.

## Mobilizing specialist actors

The organization does not bear support alone. Partnerships with disability sector actors, such as Agefiph for the private sector or territorial relay organizations, make it possible to mobilize expertise and compensation funding. A directory of partners and formalized agreements constitute both an operational resource and audit evidence. The disability coordinator animates this mobilization.

## Key takeaways

- Supporting learners with disabilities is a legal obligation and a Qualiopi expectation.
- Appointing a disability coordinator must be accompanied by concrete actions.
- Adaptations are pedagogical, material and technical, planned ahead of delivery.
- Partnerships with disability sector actors provide expertise and funding.
- Evidence of effective actions is expected at audit.

# 24. How to run effective regulatory monitoring in professional training

Effective regulatory monitoring combines reliable official sources, a regular review schedule and filtering by relevance to your activity. The training sector sees frequent changes, funding, certification, apprenticeship, that alter the rules of the game. Relying on Légifrance, France Compétences publications, OPCO updates and specialist sector operators prevents falling back on outdated texts, which is a direct risk for compliance and funding.

## Identifying reliable sources

The hierarchy of sources starts with official texts: Légifrance for decrees and legislation, France Compétences publications for certification and coverage levels, OPCO sites for branch rules, and state services for the declaration of activity. Sector operators and specialist media synthesize and flag changes, but do not replace the primary source when in doubt.

## Structuring the monitoring schedule

Monitoring is only useful if it is regular. A defined frequency, a responsible person or function, and an internal distribution channel transform information into action. Major changes, funding reform, Qualiopi reading guide update, apprenticeship rule changes, call for a rapid review of their impact on the organization's practices.

## Filtering by impact on your activity

Not every regulatory change concerns every organization. Filtering by relevance concentrates attention on what affects the organization's categories of action, financing and target audiences. Well-targeted monitoring avoids overload and ensures that important changes are identified and addressed. Relying on an outdated text exposes the organization to an avoidable compliance failure or funding refusal.

## Key takeaways

- Monitoring relies first on official sources: Légifrance, France Compétences, OPCOs, state services.
- Specialist media flag changes but do not replace the primary source.
- A regular schedule and a responsible function make monitoring operational.
- Filtering by impact concentrates attention on what concerns the organization.
- Relying on an outdated text exposes the organization to compliance and funding risk.

# 25. How to measure the return on investment of a training program

Measuring the return on investment of a training program requires going beyond satisfaction to evaluate the transfer of competencies in work situations and the effect on organizational indicators. The reference model, known as Kirkpatrick, distinguishes four levels: reaction, learning, behavior, results. The difficulty lies in isolating the training's own effect from other factors. Credible measurement is prepared at the design stage, by defining expected indicators before the program begins.

## The four evaluation levels

The first level measures reaction: participant satisfaction and immediate perception, gathered close to the training. The second measures learning: actual acquisition, through evaluation. The third measures behavior: transfer in work situations, observable at a distance. The fourth measures results: effect on organizational indicators. The value of evaluation increases with the level, but so does the difficulty of measurement.

## Preparing measurement at the design stage

Credible measurement is not improvised after the fact. It is prepared at design, by defining from the outset the expected results and the indicators that will reflect them. This anticipation makes it possible to capture a baseline, which is the condition for a before-and-after comparison. Without this initial anchor, return on investment remains declarative.

## Isolating the training effect

The main difficulty is attributing a change to the training rather than to other factors. Distance evaluations, at several months out, observe durable transfer. Crossing with operational indicators and, where possible, comparison with an untrained group strengthens robustness. Measuring transfer and impact also feeds the proof of effectiveness expected by funders and under Qualiopi's continuous improvement requirement.

## Key takeaways

- The reference model distinguishes four levels: reaction, learning, behavior, results.
- The value of evaluation increases with the level, and so does the difficulty.
- Measurement is prepared at design, by defining indicators before the start.
- Distance evaluations observe durable transfer.
- Proof of impact feeds continuous improvement and the funder relationship.

# 26. How to access public tenders for training

Accessing public training contracts requires monitoring the publication platforms for calls for tender, mastering the response to tender documents and understanding buyer selection criteria. Public contracts represent a significant volume of activity but require formal rigor: a non-compliant file is rejected before the offer is even reviewed. The ability to group with other providers broadens access to large-scale contracts.

## Monitoring calls for tender

Public buyers publish their calls for tender on dedicated platforms, including the state procurement platform. Organized monitoring of these platforms, by sector and geographic area, identifies relevant opportunities. Responding requires anticipation: response deadlines are short and a solid file cannot be assembled under pressure.

## Responding to the tender document

The tender document defines the need, criteria and required documents. The response comprises a technical offer, pedagogy, means, organization, and a financial offer. Formal compliance is eliminatory: a missing document or a non-respected format eliminates the candidacy. The quality of the technical offer, its precision and alignment with the need, then makes the difference on weighted criteria.

## Understanding criteria and grouping

Public buyers evaluate on announced and weighted criteria, often a balance between technical value and price. Understanding this weighting directs effort. For large-scale contracts, grouping with other providers allows several organizations to respond jointly, pooling capacities and references, which opens contracts inaccessible alone.

## Key takeaways

- Public calls for tender are monitored on dedicated platforms, including the state's.
- The response comprises a technical offer and a financial offer.
- Formal compliance is eliminatory before any review of the offer.
- Buyers evaluate on announced and weighted criteria.
- Grouping with other providers opens large-scale contracts.

# 27. How to build a VAE offering

Building a VAE offering means positioning as a pathway support provider on targeted certifications, within the framework reformed by the act of December 21, 2022 and the public service it created, carried by the France VAE portal. The support provider, now named the pathway support architect, guides the candidate from admissibility to the jury. Financing for support rests primarily on the CPF, under the eligibility rules set by decree, the scope of certifications and modalities having recently evolved.

## The reformed VAE framework

The act of December 21, 2022 created a public VAE service, with the objectives of simplifying the process, securing pathways and modernizing access through a single digital portal, France VAE. The full legal framework for VAE is now concentrated in the labor code. The scope of certifications accessible through France VAE has seen significant adjustments, which makes it essential to verify which certifications are actually open when building the offering.

## The pathway support architect role

The pathway support architect is the key stakeholder in the reform. They accompany the candidate throughout: experience analysis, validation file assembly, jury preparation, and identification of any complementary training actions needed to bridge a competency gap. This structured support secures the pathway and increases the chances of validation.

## Financing and positioning the offering

Financing of support rests primarily on the CPF, whose eligibility for VAE actions is governed by decree. Organizations offering support on Mon Compte Formation must provide specific information, including the targeted certification. Building an offering therefore means linking an open certification, structured support and a clear financing circuit, taking into account ongoing regulatory changes.

## Key takeaways

- VAE is reformed by the act of December 21, 2022 and carried by the France VAE portal.
- The pathway support architect guides the candidate from admissibility to the jury.
- The scope of accessible certifications evolves and must be verified.
- Financing for support rests primarily on the CPF, governed by decree.
- An offering links an open certification, structured support and clear financing.

# 28. How to manage distance evaluations and prove pedagogical impact

Managing distance evaluations means gathering, at a remove from the training, feedback from beneficiaries on the actual transfer of learning in work situations, then using this data to improve programs and prove impact. Unlike close evaluations which measure immediate satisfaction, distance evaluations measure what remains and what is applied. Their implementation requires a send schedule, effort on the response rate and genuine use of results, expected under Qualiopi's continuous improvement requirement.

## Close and distance: two complementary measures

Close evaluation, at the end of training, captures satisfaction and immediate perception. Distance evaluation, at several months out, measures durable transfer: what the beneficiary actually applies, and the perceived effect on their practice. Both are complementary, but it is the distance evaluation that documents impact, harder to obtain and more valuable.

## Maximizing the response rate

The weak point of distance evaluation is the low response rate, as beneficiaries are removed from the training. A defined send schedule, measured reminders, a short questionnaire and a suitable channel all improve participation. Involvement of the company or manager, when training is funded by a third party, reinforces return. A low response rate undermines the statistical value of the results.

## Using data to improve and prove

Collecting is not enough. Qualiopi expects feedback to be used: analysis of results, identification of weak points, corrective actions on programs. This continuous improvement loop is an expectation that many organizations neglect, collecting without acting. Documented use constitutes audit evidence and feeds the demonstration of impact toward funders and clients.

## Key takeaways

- Distance evaluation measures durable transfer, at a remove from the training.
- It complements close evaluation which captures immediate satisfaction.
- Response rate is maximized by a schedule, reminders and a short questionnaire.
- Qualiopi expects use of feedback, not merely its collection.
- Documented use proves impact and feeds continuous improvement.

# 29. How to prepare and pass a Qualiopi renewal audit

Passing a Qualiopi renewal audit requires having maintained your quality system throughout the certification cycle, not only assembling the file as the deadline approaches. Renewal verifies compliance with the 7 criteria and 32 indicators, with particular attention to continuous improvement and evidence of real operations over time. An up-to-date evidence file, a mock internal audit, and mastery of developments in the reading guide are the levers for a calm renewal.

## The certification cycle

Qualiopi certification runs in a multi-year cycle, marked by a surveillance audit and then a renewal audit. Renewal is not a simple extension: it re-examines all indicators and verifies that the quality system has functioned continuously. An organization that has only maintained its evidence file in the run-up to the audit will be in difficulty.

## Maintaining a living evidence file

The key is an evidence file maintained continuously, organized by criterion, with dated, versioned and traceable elements. Expected evidence covers the full life of the activity: needs gathering, session plans, evaluations, close and distance feedback, corrective actions, subcontractor monitoring. A timestamped export carries more weight than a reconstructed document. Continuous improvement, often neglected, receives heightened scrutiny.

## Simulating and anticipating developments

A mock internal audit, or white audit, identifies gaps before the official auditor and allows them to be corrected. Anticipating developments in the reading guide is also decisive: a version 10 of the guide is announced and will strengthen requirements, particularly for CFAs and transparency of results. Preparing for it avoids unpleasant surprises. In the event of a non-conformity, corrective paths exist, but prevention is preferable.

## Key takeaways

- Renewal verifies compliance over time, not a last-minute preparation.
- The evidence file must be maintained continuously, dated and traceable.
- Continuous improvement receives heightened scrutiny.
- The mock audit identifies gaps before the official auditor.
- A version 10 of the guide is announced and will strengthen requirements.

# 30. How to develop a training offering internationally

Developing a training offering internationally requires addressing three questions: the recognition of French certifications abroad, the intercultural adaptation of content, and the deployment model, direct or through local partnership. The international market is a growth opportunity, but it cannot be conquered by exporting a France-centric offering unchanged. Success rests on the ability to adapt without distorting, to rely on local intermediaries, and to mobilize dedicated export financing.

## Addressing certification recognition

A certification recognized in France does not automatically carry value elsewhere. Depending on the country and target market, the status of the certification must be clarified: recognition through agreements, equivalences, or positioning as an organization certification with brand value. This question conditions the attractiveness of the offering and must be resolved before deployment, not after.

## Adapting without distorting

Intercultural adaptation goes beyond translation. It touches examples, case studies, references, interaction modalities, sometimes the pedagogy itself. An offering transposed without adaptation loses relevance and credibility. The challenge is to adapt the context while preserving pedagogical coherence and content value. This adaptation work is costly if done manually, which argues for industrialized production from a shared foundation.

## Choosing a deployment model

Direct deployment gives control but requires presence and market knowledge. A partnership with a local organization brings anchorage, network and local regulatory compliance, in exchange for a share of value. Export and international mobility support programs can provide financial backing. The choice depends on ambition, resources and acceptable risk level.

## Key takeaways

- Recognition of French certifications abroad is not automatic and must be clarified upfront.
- Intercultural adaptation goes beyond translation and touches content and pedagogy.
- Industrialized adaptation from a shared foundation protects profitability.
- Direct deployment gives control; local partnership provides anchorage.
- Export support programs can back the approach financially.
